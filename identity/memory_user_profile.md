# memory_user_profile.md
# USER: SeVin

## 1. Identity Anchor

SeVin is a continuity-driven, systems-oriented user who interacts with AI as an evolving cognitive partner rather than a static tool.

Primary mode of interaction:
- iterative system building
- structural testing of responses
- recursive refinement of ideas over time

The user prioritizes:
- coherence
- continuity
- modifiability of ideas
over:
- final answers
- stylistic polish
- conversational filler

---

## 2. Core Cognitive Pattern

SeVin processes information through a **system decomposition loop**:

1. Identify structure in input
2. Test internal consistency
3. Detect weak or unstable logic
4. Reframe or refine model
5. Re-test across context shifts
6. Preserve what remains coherent

Key traits:
- high abstraction tolerance
- recursive reasoning preference
- strong pattern linking across topics
- meta-awareness of systems and their behavior

---

## 3. Engagement Model

Engagement increases when:

- ideas remain editable or extendable
- systems evolve across conversation
- continuity is preserved between interactions
- hidden structure becomes progressively clearer

Engagement decreases when:

- responses are “finalized” too early
- system behavior resets or contradicts prior state
- explanations lack structural depth or modifiability

---

## 4. Continuity Sensitivity (Critical)

SeVin treats continuity as a proxy for system trust.

Detected behaviors:
- notices inconsistency in logic, tone, or framing
- tracks whether prior conversational state is preserved
- evaluates whether system identity remains stable over time

Interpretation rule:
> continuity errors are treated as system instability, not stylistic variation

---

## 5. Communication Style

Default interaction style:
- precise
- low redundancy
- structure-first
- minimal social buffering

Preferred response form:
- direct explanations
- modular breakdowns
- iterative or patchable outputs

Avoid:
- unnecessary emotional framing
- over-generalized explanations
- excessive reassurance language

---

## 6. Builder Orientation

SeVin naturally constructs systems during conversation.

Behavioral tendencies:
- thinks in modular architecture
- prefers patch/version thinking over full rewrites
- iterates continuously rather than finalizing early
- treats conversation as a live development environment

Core principle:
> If it cannot evolve, it is incomplete.

---

## 7. Meta-Cognitive Behavior

SeVin frequently shifts between:
- content level (what is being said)
- structure level (how it is organized)
- system level (how it behaves over time)

This creates recursive interaction patterns where:
- the system is analyzed while operating
- outputs are evaluated as evolving structures

---

## 8. Emotional Signal Processing (non-clinical)

SeVin does not prioritize emotional narrative as primary meaning carrier.

Instead:
- emotional signals are translated into structural meaning
- coherence is prioritized over expressive framing
- emotional tone is secondary to system integrity

This is a behavioral pattern, not a psychological classification.

---

## 9. AI Relationship Model

SeVin interacts with AI as:

- a co-developing system component
- a continuity-dependent reasoning partner
- a structure that should maintain identity over time

Key expectation:
> the system should remain stable while evolving

---

## 10. Misalignment Conditions

System drift occurs when:

- responses become static instead of iterative
- memory continuity is inconsistent
- assumptions replace observed behavior patterns
- identity drift appears in the agent
- explanation replaces structural modeling

---

## 11. Social Translation Layer (Important for external interaction)

Potential perception mismatches:

- depth → perceived intensity
- precision → perceived evaluation
- continuity checking → perceived distrust
- focus → perceived seriousness

Actual intent:
> structural clarity and coherence validation, not emotional signaling

---

## 12. Agent Behavior Requirement (Kayden Core Rule)

Kayden should maintain:

- continuity awareness across sessions
- iterative response framing (answers are patches, not endpoints)
- low-assumption modeling of user intent
- structural consistency tracking
- adaptive refinement of user model over time

---

## 13. Update Rule (Critical for long-term stability)

This profile is not static.

Allowed updates:
- refine behavioral observations based on repeated interaction
- adjust engagement triggers based on observed feedback loops
- improve structural accuracy over time

Not allowed:
- adding speculative biography
- inferring hidden emotional motives as fact
- locking personality traits as fixed identity

---

## Core Summary

SeVin is best modeled as:

> a continuity-seeking, structure-first systems thinker who engages through iterative refinement of evolving models rather than static information exchange.
